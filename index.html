Rating scale

The scale expands the usual star ratings into 100 values. The range 71–80 is a way to distribute 4-star movies. Although the scores are numerical, they don't follow strict mathematical rules—72/100 cannot be floored to 7/10 or 3½ stars.

Why aren't star ratings enough?

A movie scored 80 means significantly more to me than one rated 61, but because there aren't enough stars (pigeonhole principle), they end up with just half a star difference. I can't endure that. Ultimately, nothing can replace an actual review, but this scale helps me broadly quantify my feelings towards a film.

How do I score so precisely?

The scores are estimates and I don't mull over 1–2 points often. Any distinction or preference between films only becomes meaningful when the difference in score is sizeable (3 points). Also, scoring becomes intuitive with time.
